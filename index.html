CORE CLAIM

THE DATASET IS CONSTRUCTED, NOT SIMPLY COLLECTED.

LAION-5B is produced through web infrastructure, platform conventions, machine-learning models, and statistical rules before it becomes training data.

  1. 01
    AUTHOR ARGUMENT

    AUTOMATION RELOCATES HUMAN JUDGMENT.

    At web scale, curation does not disappear. Decisions about what belongs are displaced into models, scores, classifications, and thresholds.

    AUTOMATED CURATION / RESPONSIBILITY GAP / STATISTICAL DECISION-MAKING
  2. 02
    AUTHOR ARGUMENT

    THE DATASET REPRESENTS THE WEB, NOT THE WORLD.

    LAION-5B reflects the parts of the web that can be crawled, parsed, and made machine-readable, including the commercial and platform logics embedded in ALT text.

    WEB INFRASTRUCTURE / PLATFORM MEDIATION / MACHINE-READABLE CULTURE
  3. 03
    AUTHOR ARGUMENT

    TECHNICAL SCORES BECOME CULTURAL DECISIONS.

    Similarity, language, aesthetics, safety, and ownership are translated into model-generated scores and categories that determine what remains visible.

    THRESHOLD POLITICS / QUANTIFIED JUDGMENT / METRICIZATION
  4. 04
    AUTHOR ARGUMENT

    MODELS AND DATASETS FORM A RECURSIVE STACK.

    Models help construct datasets that train later models. Assumptions and blind spots can travel through these layers and become increasingly difficult to locate.

    MODEL RECURSION / INFRASTRUCTURAL OPACITY / EPISTEMIC CIRCULARITY
  5. 05
    AUTHOR ARGUMENT

    TRANSPARENCY ENABLES INSPECTION, NOT ACCOUNTABILITY.

    LAION’s openness makes public investigation possible, but access alone does not make a dataset safe, fair, or responsibly governed.

    AUDITABILITY / GOVERNANCE / TRANSPARENCY ≠ ACCOUNTABILITY
PERSONAL ASSESSMENT

The project makes scale visible, but its long-form system map can still be difficult to hold in view as a whole.
